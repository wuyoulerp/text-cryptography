RSA

Short for Rivest-Shamir-Adleman (the creators). It's a public key encryption algorithm, meaning it has two keys (private and public).
Receiver's public key is used to encrypt the file, and the receiver's private key is used by the receiver to decrypt the file.
Typically used in digital signatures and certificates, as well as secure communication protocols (TLS/SSL). More often used to encrypt symmetric keys, because it's slow on large files.

AES

Short for Advanced Encryption Standard, was known as Rijndael (the creators; Rijmen and Daemen from Belgium).
Created as a successor to NIST's Data Encryption Standard (DES) because it can be brute-forced. NIST made an open competition and Rijndael was chosen.
It's a private key encryption algorithm, meaning it only has one key for encryption and decryption.
Widely used in real life, from encrypted browsing, securing wi-fi networks, cloud storages, messaging, and disk encryption.

ROT13

Also known as "Caesar cipher shift 13" because what it does is shift alphabets by 13 letters (a -> n).
This cipher is easily reversible, it shouldn't be used for important data like passwords (please NEVER do that or I will revoke your coding privileges).
